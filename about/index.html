About Me

Cybersecurity enthusiast on a journey to master ethical hacking

My Journey

The overall interest in the field of technology stemmed from my computer science academic days. I got acquainted with both software development and the very exciting world of cybersecurity—especially cryptography and network security. What first piqued my curiosity was the unseen fight between the defenders and the attackers on the cyber battleground. Meanwhile, I was enjoying the whole process of software development—transforming notions into runnable applications through neat logic and coding. Both seemed like playing with endless puzzles to me, and therefore I wanted to be a master of both rather than choosing just one. After that point in time, I let curiosity, experimentation, and hands-on learning be my guides throughout the journey. I allocate a huge amount of my time to creating tiny software pieces, writing and perfecting code, and comprehending how systems operate from the inside. Besides this, I am also involved in probing for security flaws, CTF competitions, and studies that focus on how software can be smashed and most importantly, how it can be fortified. Each project and competition reinforces my capability to think like both a developer and a defender at the same time.


Presently, I am concentrating on practical learning that is close to real-world scenarios in both areas. In the cybersecurity domain, I am gathering technical know-how in penetration testing, the hardening of Linux security, and automated security using Python. On the software side, my endeavors are to improve my programming basics, create large-scale applications, and write code that is secure, maintainable, and usable. The day-to-day experience is full of new challenges which are helping me to develop both technically and psychologically. Ultimately, I envision myself as a professional who has the whole system—from its weakest to its strongest link—understood completely; a person who can not only develop software quickly and easily but also secure it properly.
